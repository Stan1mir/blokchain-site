Blockchain in Education: Securing Credentials and Empowering Learners

The traditional education system has long faced challenges with issues like credential fraud, a lack of student data privacy, and the difficulty of tracking lifelong learning achievements. Blockchain technology offers powerful solutions to these problems, promising to revolutionize education as we know it (Fatima and Singh, as cited in Avasthi et al., 2024).

Benefit 1: Authentic and Secure Credentials

Fraud in academic credentials has been a persistent issue. Blockchain addresses this by providing a decentralized and immutable record-keeping system. When a university issues a degree, it can be recorded as a digital credential on the blockchain, creating a permanent, tamper-proof record that anyone can verify.

For example, the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) developed a system called "Blockcerts" which uses blockchain to issue digital diplomas. These credentials are verifiable, secure, and entirely owned and controlled by the learner, effectively eliminating counterfeit certificates.

Benefit 2: Learner Data Privacy and Control

In the age of digital education, student data privacy is a critical concern. Blockchain provides a solution by empowering learners to have full control over their own personal information. Instead of their data being held on a school's central server, students can manage their own records on the blockchain, choosing exactly what data to share and with whom, ensuring lifelong privacy and security.

Benefit 3: Tracking Lifelong Learning

Education doesn't stop after graduation. Blockchain can enable a comprehensive and unchangeable record of a person's lifelong learning journey. Every course, certification, and skill acquired throughout a career can be recorded on the blockchain. This creates a single, transparent, and verifiable transcript that makes it easier for employers and other stakeholders to accurately assess an individual's skills and qualifications.
